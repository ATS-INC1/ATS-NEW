How to Pick a Load Board For Your Trucking Company 

Now that you've had time to digest this list of load boards, your next step is to pick one. That can be easier said than done! Even with a short list like this, youâ€™ll still need to evaluate each of them to decide whether theyâ€™ll be a good fit for your companyâ€™s needs. 

Here are three things to consider as you choose a load board: 

1. Load Board Price 

Many load boards have associated costs especially if theyâ€™re not attached to a logistics company. Most paid load boards allow users to pay monthly or annually. Many also offer a discount for users who choose to pay for a full year all at once rather than once per month.

Regardless of the billing schedule you choose, the cost of using a paid load board is a cost â€” one you'll want to weigh carefully before making your final decision. 

If you have slimmer margins (which was the case for many trucking companies throughout last year), you might want to opt for a cheaper or no-cost board.

2. Load Board Reviews

What's the word on the street? As you try to decide on a new load board, pay attention to what other users are saying.

Load boards that have a large number of poor reviews (3 stars or less) or few reviews at all probably aren't the best products. While it's true that people are typically more motivated to leave a bad review than they are a good one, it's also true that where thereâ€™s smoke, thereâ€™s usually fire. 

Look for load boards with above-average user ratings and a large volume of reviews. Read a selection of reviews across a variety of ratings to get a complete picture of the user experience for each board. 

Taking all the feedback into account â€” the good, the bad, and the 'meh' â€” will help you make a decision with your eyes wide open.

3. Brand Reputation

There is a business behind every load board, whether it's a trucking company, freight brokerage, or a tech business.

As such, it's in your best interest to do some research into whether the company that manages your top-choice load board is legitimate, reputable, and financially stable.

Look for providers with a rich history and strong industry affiliations. This is particularly important when evaluating load boards operated by logistics companies, which will require users to be onboarded as a carrier before they can book loads.
