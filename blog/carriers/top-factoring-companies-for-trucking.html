According to a May 31, 2024 snapshot of the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration’s (FMCSA’s) carrier registration statistics, there are more than two million trucking companies in the U.S. 

Many of these carriers use factoring to receive payment for their delivered loads. This helps them manage their cash flow and avoid the pain of having to wait 30-90 days before payment arrives. 

Like any trucking company, you have regular overhead expenses (fuel, maintenance, insurance, etc.) you need to pay. That’s why you need consistent cash flow. Working with a factoring company has pros and cons, but it will certainly get you paid faster. 

That said, you can only use one factoring provider at a time. Since the best factoring companies can become a seamless extension of your business and make your life easier, you’ll want to choose wisely. 

At Anderson Trucking Service (ATS), our freight brokerage company ATS Logistics Services, Inc. works with thousands of carriers. The majority of those carriers (more than 75 percent) use factoring companies when hauling loads with us. 

This article lists 15 of the factoring companies many ATS Logistics carriers use to receive payment promptly. We’re familiar with them and how they do business, so we feel confident recommending them to you as possible partners for your trucking company.

Check out our 15 top picks below to learn more about each company, their factoring services, and what reviewers are saying.

NOTE: Companies are listed in alphabetical order. The ratings and review counts listed below are accurate at the time of publication. Ratings and review counts change frequently and may have fluctuated since our last update.

How to Choose a Factoring Company

Choosing the right factoring company is a critical decision for your trucking business. With over two million trucking companies in the U.S., maintaining a healthy cash flow is essential to staying competitive in the market, as it allows you to cover regular overhead expenses and stay in the green.

Factoring offers a tidy solution by providing immediate payment for delivered loads, helping carriers manage their finances more effectively. 

Each company on this list offers unique features and services, from fair factoring rates and dedicated account representatives to convenient mobile apps and value-add perks. 

Reviewing these companies’ histories, service offerings, and customer ratings is a great starting place that can help you identify the best fit for your business needs. 

When selecting a factoring company, you’ll want to carefully weigh each prospect’s experience, reputation, and transparency regarding contract terms and fees. Doing your due diligence ensures you partner with a reliable company that supports your business operations seamlessly. 

It’s also possible that factoring isn’t the right financial solution for your business. If you’re still not sure whether factoring will be a good fit, check out What is a Factoring Company? (How to Select One as a Carrier).

This article breaks down factoring even further, outlining its potential business benefits, its disadvantages, and offering detailed advice on how to choose a factoring company that meets your needs. 

Use this resource as a tool that can help you make an informed decision — one that could ultimately enhance your trucking business's financial stability and growth.
